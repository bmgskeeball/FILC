What is Arcade Rolling?

Arcade Rolling is the evolved, regulated form of a classic arcade game. While often referred to as skeeball in the United States, the competitive sport is distinct in both rules and regulation. Played on certified lanes with league-grade equipment, it has grown into a ranked international sport with formal titles, seasonal tours, and a dedicated governing body.

Regional Names and Variants

Though globally governed by FILC, Arcade Rolling is known by many names:

  • Starball in Finland
  • Spin Bowling in parts of Central Europe
  • Boardwalk Rolling in the United States
  • Cible-Roulement in Qu√©bec

Older variants include the 5-ring format, now largely retired. The official competitive discipline uses seven scoring cups ("seven-ring") and is internationally standardized by FILC.

Rollmasters & Rankings

The highest honor in the sport is Rollmaster status, awarded for major championship wins, perfect frames, or elite multi-season performance. While FILC governs the global Rollmaster list, many national and regional circuits maintain their own rankings, sometimes leading to multiple competing designations.
