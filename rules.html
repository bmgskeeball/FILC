Rules & Regulations

The FILC maintains the official international ruleset for Arcade Rolling, also known as Seven-Ring Bowling. This page summarizes core gameplay standards, match formats, and equipment regulations. Download the full rulebook below for tournament details and classifications.

Basic Match Format

  • Each match consists of 10 frames
  • Each frame includes 9 balls
  • Lowest-scoring frame is dropped
  • Maximum possible score: 900 per frame, 8100 per match
  • Scores displayed as two-digit shorthand (e.g., 650 = 65)

Scoring Rings

  • 2 x 100-point corner pockets ("Simpson Pockets")
  • 1 x 50-point ring
  • 1 x 40-point ring
  • 1 x 30-point ring
  • 1 x 20-point ring
  • 1 x 10-point ring
  • Outer lane: 0 points (known as a "zilch" or "cero")

Gameplay & Fouls

  • Players must release the ball before crossing the foul line
  • Crossing the line results in a "strike" â€” the roll is void
  • Competitors may challenge strikes similar to a "check swing" in baseball
  • Ties are broken with a bonus frame sudden-death roll
  • Electronic foul detection required at elite events
