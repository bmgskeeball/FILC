Understanding Rankings

FILC does not publish a global player leaderboard. Instead, we maintain a dynamic analytics system used to rank players within sanctioned tours and national circuits, with a focus on performance, scoring consistency, and competitive tier.

Scoring Standards

Arcade Rolling matches are scored out of 900 per frame. Matches are typically 10 frames, with the lowest score droppedâ€”making the max possible match score 8100 (displayed as "810").

Scores are often written without the trailing zero (e.g., a 540 frame is notated as "54") for clarity in competition summaries. Tournament scorecards always use the full value.

Simpson Points & Ranking Calculations

The world ranking system is based on Simpson Points, awarded based on event tier and finishing position. Points are calculated from a player's best 12 events over a rolling 24-month period. Grand Prix and Major events are weighted more heavily than Challenger events.

Bonus Simpson Points are awarded for Perfect Frames, Diamond Rings (5 consecutive 800+ games), and tournament-high frame scores.

Rollmaster Designation

The title of Rollmaster is awarded to players who:

  • Win a FILC Major Championship
  • Achieve a Perfect Frame (900 points)
  • Win three or more Grand Prix events

This honor is permanent and does not expire. Each country maintains its own list of national Rollmasters. Some regional circuits or rival associations also maintain Rollmaster titles, resulting in occasional overlap or contention.

Analytics and Metrics

FILC publishes limited advanced stats, but many federations and fans use deeper metrics to evaluate player performance:

  • Frame Average (FA): Mean score across all official frames
  • Peak Frame: Highest single frame in competition
  • Zero Rate: Percentage of rolls scoring zero (Zilch)
  • Strike Rate: Frequency of foot faults or illegal deliveries
  • Consistency Index: Standard deviation of frame scores across a match or season

Advanced analytics platforms like Savant.Roll and FrameLab provide tools for deeper match analysis, including opponent-adjusted scores and lane condition factors.
